Sea Otters

The Floating Teddy Bears of the Ocean

Meet the Sea Otter!

Sea otters are amazing marine mammals that live in the Pacific Ocean. They have the thickest fur of any animal - up to one million hairs per square inch! This incredible fur coat keeps them warm in cold ocean waters. Unlike other marine mammals, sea otters don't have blubber for insulation.

Life on Their Backs

Sea otters spend most of their lives floating on their backs! They eat, sleep, and even give birth while floating. They use kelp as an anchor, wrapping themselves in it so they don't drift away while sleeping. Groups of otters floating together are called 'rafts' - sometimes over 100 otters float together!

Ocean's Tool Users

Sea otters are one of the few animals that use tools! They use rocks to crack open shellfish like clams and sea urchins. Each otter has a favorite rock that they keep in a pouch of loose skin under their armpit. They place the rock on their chest and smash the shellfish against it - nature's dinner table!

Kelp Forest Guardians

Sea otters are ecosystem heroes! By eating sea urchins, they protect kelp forests from being destroyed. Without otters, sea urchins would eat all the kelp, turning vibrant underwater forests into barren areas. Kelp forests provide homes for thousands of species and help fight climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide.

Adorable Otter Pups

Baby sea otters, called pups, are born in the water and can float immediately! Their fluffy fur is so buoyant they can't dive underwater for the first few weeks. Mother otters are incredibly devoted, carrying their pups on their bellies for months, teaching them to swim, dive, and find food. The bond between mother and pup is one of the strongest in the animal kingdom!

Amazing Otter Facts!

🍽️

Sea otters eat 25% of their body weight every day - that's like a kid eating 40 hamburgers!

🤝

Otters hold hands while sleeping so they don't drift apart!

🦷

Their teeth are purple! The purple color comes from eating so many purple sea urchins.

💨

They can blow air into their fur to stay extra floaty - like wearing a life jacket!
