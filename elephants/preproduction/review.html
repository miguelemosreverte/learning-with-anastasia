Preface

Welcome to the magnificent world of African elephants! In this adventure, we'll follow Tembo, a curious young elephant calf, as he takes his first wobbly steps on the golden savanna and learns everything from his loving family.

Elephants are among the most intelligent animals on Earth. They mourn their dead, use tools, communicate over vast distances, and form family bonds that last a lifetime. Their herds are led by wise matriarchs who remember water sources from decades past.

Through Tembo's first year, you'll discover how elephants shape entire landscapes by pushing trees and digging for water, how they protect each other through the darkest nights, and why these gentle giants truly deserve to be called nature's greatest engineers!

Viewer Details

The Amazing Trunk

Look at Tembo's trunk tip — see those two finger-like projections? African elephants have two 'fingers' at the tip of their trunk (Asian elephants have just one). With over 40,000 muscles, the trunk can pick up something as tiny as a single blade of grass or as heavy as a tree branch!

Shock-Absorbing Feet

An elephant's foot hides a remarkable secret — a thick, spongy pad of fatty tissue that acts like a natural shock absorber! This pad spreads their enormous weight evenly and lets them walk almost silently despite weighing up to 6 tons. That's why elephants can sneak up on you!

Built-In Air Conditioning

See the network of blood vessels in Tembo's ears? An elephant's ears are filled with a web of blood vessels just beneath the thin skin. When it's hot, blood flows through these vessels and heat radiates out through the large ear surface. By flapping their ears, elephants can cool their blood by up to 5°C!

Tusk Growth Rings

Just like trees have growth rings, elephant tusks have rings too! By studying these rings, scientists can learn an elephant's age, health, and even what food was available in different years. Grandmother Nyota's broken tusk shows rings from sixty years of savanna life.

Fun Facts

Hearing Through Their Feet

Elephants can detect seismic vibrations through the ground using special sensors in their feet! They can 'hear' other elephants' calls from up to 10 miles away through the earth, long before the sound reaches their ears through the air.

Elephant Hugs

Elephants hug each other with their trunks! When two elephants meet, they wrap their trunks together in a warm greeting — just like people shaking hands or giving a hug. Baby elephants even hold their mama's tail with their trunk while walking, like a toddler holding their parent's hand!

Trunk-Sucking Babies

Just like human babies suck their thumbs for comfort, baby elephants suck on the tips of their trunks! It calms them down and helps them feel safe. They usually grow out of this habit by age two, just like human toddlers stop sucking their thumbs.

Swimming Champions

Did you know elephants are amazing swimmers? They can swim for hours without stopping, using their trunk as a snorkel to breathe while their body is completely underwater! Baby elephants learn to swim when they are very young, and they love splashing in deep water with their families. Some elephants have even been spotted swimming between islands!
