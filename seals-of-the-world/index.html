Seals of the World

Amazing swimmers from every ocean!

Masters of the Ocean

From Arctic Ice to Tropical Beaches

Meet the Harbor Seal!

Harbor seals are the puppies of the sea! They live near coastlines all around the northern parts of our planet. These playful friends love to rest on rocks and beaches, warming up in the sunshine.

With their spotted coats and big, curious eyes, harbor seals are excellent swimmers. They can hold their breath for up to 30 minutes and dive as deep as 500 feet to catch fish!

The Giant Elephant Seal

Elephant seals are the giants of the seal world! Male elephant seals can weigh as much as four cars put together! They get their name from the male's huge nose that looks like an elephant's trunk.

These amazing divers are the champions of deep diving. They can dive deeper than any other seal - over a mile deep! That's like stacking five Eiffel Towers on top of each other!

The Speedy Leopard Seal

Leopard seals are the race cars of the Antarctic waters! With their spotted coats that look like a leopard's fur, these seals are incredible swimmers who can zoom through the water at 25 miles per hour!

They have the longest, most powerful jaws of any seal, which they use to catch fish and squid. Leopard seals are also known for their beautiful underwater songs that sound like they're from another world!

The Smiling Weddell Seal

Weddell seals are the happiest-looking seals in Antarctica! Their faces have a natural upward curve that makes them look like they're always smiling. These champion divers live further south than any other mammal!

They're amazing at making breathing holes in the ice using their strong teeth. Weddell seals can stay underwater for over an hour, exploring the magical world beneath the Antarctic ice!

The Tropical Monk Seal

Hawaiian monk seals are the only seals that live in warm, tropical waters! While their cousins love cold water, these special seals enjoy the sunny beaches of Hawaii, where they nap on the warm sand.

They got their name because the fold of skin on their heads looks like a monk's hood. These peaceful seals love to rest during the day and hunt for fish, octopuses, and lobsters at night!

The Adorable Harp Seal

Harp seal babies are some of the cutest animals on Earth! They're born with fluffy white coats that keep them warm on the ice. As they grow up, they develop a beautiful harp-shaped pattern on their backs!

These amazing travelers swim thousands of miles every year between their summer and winter homes. Harp seals are fantastic swimmers and can do backflips and spins in the water like underwater acrobats!

The Fluffy Fur Seal

Fur seals have the softest, thickest fur coats in the seal family! They have two layers of fur that keep them cozy in cold water - it's like wearing a warm sweater and a raincoat at the same time!

Unlike true seals, fur seals can walk on all four flippers, which makes them great climbers on rocky shores. They love to play together and can often be seen surfing in the waves just for fun!

🏊 Amazing Fact: Underwater Sleepers!

Seals can sleep underwater! They have a special reflex that makes them surface to breathe even while sleeping. They can float vertically in the water with just their noses poking out, taking breaths without even waking up!

👁️ Amazing Fact: Super Underwater Vision!

Seals can see perfectly underwater but are a bit nearsighted on land - like the opposite of us! Their eyes are specially designed with extra-large pupils and a special layer that helps them see in the dark depths of the ocean.

🎵 Amazing Fact: Voice Recognition!

Each seal has its own unique call, like a voice fingerprint, so moms and babies can find each other! Even in colonies with thousands of seals, a mother can find her pup by its special call from hundreds of feet away!

❄️ Amazing Fact: Heart Rate Champions!

Some seals can slow their heartbeat from 100 to 4 beats per minute when diving! This incredible ability helps them save oxygen and stay underwater for over an hour while hunting for fish in the deep ocean.
